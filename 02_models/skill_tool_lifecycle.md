# Model — Skill & Tool Lifecycle

## Core Idea

Every age transition creates three categories of skills and tools:

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| **Obsolete** | Replaced by the new age's solutions — still functional, no longer competitive |
| **Emerging** | New skills/tools that the new scarcity demands — rare at first, then dominant |
| **Evergreen** | Remain valuable across every age — they solve problems that never fully disappear |

> The same pattern repeats: what was once rare becomes standard, then becomes invisible, then becomes obsolete — unless it is evergreen.

---

## The Lifecycle Pattern

```
Emerging → Dominant → Commoditized → Obsolete
                              ↘ (some) → Evergreen
```

- **Emerging:** Practiced by a few, high leverage, poorly understood by most
- **Dominant:** Core skill/tool of the age — widespread, actively taught
- **Commoditized:** Assumed baseline — no longer a differentiator
- **Obsolete:** Replaced by a better solution — effort no longer worth the return
- **Evergreen:** Never obsolete — the constraint they solve is permanent (coordination, meaning, trust)

---

## Obsolescence Per Age Transition

| Transition | Obsolete Skills | Obsolete Tools |
|---|---|---|
| Survival → Agriculture | Nomadic foraging, seasonal migration patterns | Temporary shelters, pure hunting rigs |
| Agriculture → Civilization | Every-person-farms (specialization begins) | Small-scale hand tools (superseded by organized labor) |
| Civilization → Industrial | Scribes, artisan craft at scale, manual calculation | Hand looms, manual mills, clay tablet records |
| Industrial → Energy | Steam engineers (narrow), telegraph operators | Steam-only machinery, manual typesetting |
| Energy → Information | Physical filing, memorization as primary knowledge store | Card catalogs, physical encyclopedias, fax machines |
| Information → Alignment | Rote coding, data entry, search-based research, memorizing syntax | Manual dashboards, standalone software silos |

---

## Emerging Per Age Transition

| Transition | Emerging Skills | Emerging Tools |
|---|---|---|
| Survival → Agriculture | Farming, crop rotation, animal husbandry | Plow, granary, irrigation |
| Agriculture → Civilization | Writing, law, arithmetic, governance | Writing systems, money, roads |
| Civilization → Industrial | Machine operation, engineering, management | Steam engine, factory, railway |
| Industrial → Energy | Civil/electrical engineering, logistics, finance | Power grid, oil infrastructure, telegraphy |
| Energy → Information | Programming, data analysis, digital literacy | Computer, internet, software |
| Information → Alignment | Systems thinking, AI orchestration, judgment, taste | AI assistants, automation frameworks, decision tools |

---

## Evergreen Skills

These skills have never become obsolete. They scale across every age.

| Skill | Why It Persists |
|---|---|
| Communication & storytelling | Coordination always requires shared meaning |
| Leadership & social influence | Human groups always need someone to align toward a goal |
| Judgment under uncertainty | Every age has decisions that cannot be computed |
| Teaching & knowledge transfer | Every new age requires bringing others along |
| Negotiation | Scarcity creates conflict; conflict requires resolution |
| Pattern recognition | The nature of the constraint changes; the skill of seeing it does not |
| Curiosity & learning | Each new age requires learning what did not exist before |

---

## Evergreen Tools

These tools persist across ages, though they evolve in form.

| Tool | Why It Persists |
|---|---|
| Language / writing | Coordination at any scale requires recorded meaning |
| Mathematics | Every age involves measurement, trade, or optimization |
| Maps / spatial models | Navigation — physical or conceptual — never goes away |
| Money / exchange systems | Scarcity always produces trade |
| Governance structures | Scale always creates the need to coordinate power |

---

## Key Insight

> Evergreen skills are not the most valuable skills in any one age.
> They are the most valuable skills *across* ages — the ones that compound.

A person who masters an emerging skill rides the wave of one age.
A person who also holds evergreen skills is useful in every age transition.

**In the Alignment Age:** emerging skills (AI orchestration, systems thinking) are the leverage point.
But the floor is still built from evergreen skills — judgment, communication, teaching.
