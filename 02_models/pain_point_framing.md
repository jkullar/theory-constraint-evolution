# Model — Pain Point Framing

## Core Insight

The theory's two key primitives map directly to what business calls "pain points" — but at different levels of abstraction.

| Primitive | Business Equivalent | Level |
|---|---|---|
| Scarcity | Market-level pain point | Civilizational / industry |
| Coordination | Organizational pain point | Company / team / execution |

---

## Scarcity = Market-Level Pain Point

Every major industry exists because someone productized the relief of a scarcity:

| Scarcity | Industry Born From It |
|---|---|
| Food | Agriculture, food production |
| Energy | Utilities, oil, infrastructure |
| Information | Software, internet, SaaS |
| Aligned execution | (current — emerging) |

Businesses built on scarcity relief are structural — they solve a pain that *everyone* suffers, not just a segment.

---

## Coordination = Organizational Pain Point

The three coordination layers are textbook business pain points:

| Coordination Layer | Business Pain Point |
|---|---|
| Clarity — what matters? | Strategic paralysis, misaligned goals, priority conflicts |
| Who does what? | Silos, accountability gaps, mismatched incentives |
| Execution alignment | Projects stalling, teams pulling in different directions |

This is why project management, OKR tools, org design consulting, and leadership coaching are all large markets — they are all selling coordination relief.

---

## The Key Distinction

Most businesses identify **surface-level** pain points (e.g., "our onboarding is slow").

This theory identifies **structural** pain points — ones that exist because of where civilization sits in the constraint loop.

> Surface pain points are symptoms.
> Structural pain points are the cause.

A business solving a coordination pain point (e.g., project management software) is successful because the *underlying structural scarcity* — aligned execution — is at its peak.

---

## Why This Matters for Opportunity

The theory predicts which pain points are:
- **Largest right now** — those at the current constraint layer
- **Growing** — those upstream of an unsolved scarcity
- **Temporary** — those that will collapse once the next Builder wave scales
