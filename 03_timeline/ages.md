# Timeline — Ages of Human Progress

Each age is defined by one dominant scarcity. When that scarcity is solved, the next age begins.

> Old scarcities never disappear — they become cheaper, more reliable, less dominant.

---

## Age vs. Epoch

**Age** is the preferred term in this theory.

An *epoch* marks a span of time. An *age* marks a **constraint regime** — a period defined by what is scarce, what Builders are solving, and what breaks when they succeed.

The shift from one age to the next is **caused**, not just observed. That causal logic is what "age" captures and "epoch" does not.

---

## The Constraint Loop (Per Age)

Scarcity → Builders solve → Scale increases → Coordination breaks → Coordinators organize → New scarcity emerges

## Age 1 — Survival (Hunter-Gatherer Era)

**Primary scarcity:** Food, water, shelter

**Key traits:**
- Nomadic life
- Constant uncertainty
- No surplus

**Builders:** Fire, basic tools (stone, bone)

**Coordinators:** Tribal leaders, social bonds

**Skills needed:** Hunting, foraging, fire-making, tool crafting, navigation, social bonding, pattern recognition (tracking animals, seasons)

**Tools needed:** Stone tools, bone/wood implements, fire, basic traps and snares

**Obsoleted by next age:** Nomadic migration routes, pure foraging knowledge — still functional, no longer the constraint

**Outcome:** Survival stabilized → New constraint: control over land

---

## Age 2 — Agricultural Age

**Primary scarcity:** Productive land

**Key traits:**
- Settlements and villages
- Food surplus begins
- Property and ownership emerge

**Builders:** Farming systems, irrigation, domestication of animals

**Coordinators:** Property systems, early governance

**Skills needed:** Farming, animal husbandry, crop selection, irrigation management, food storage, seasonal planning, basic trade and barter

**Tools needed:** Plows, irrigation channels, granaries, domesticated animals, basic pottery and containers

**Obsoleted by next age:** Every-person-farms — specialization begins; generalist survival skills commoditized

**Outcome:** Food scarcity reduced, population increases → New constraint: organization at scale

---

## Age 3 — Civilization / Land-Control Age

**Primary scarcity:** Control of land and people

**Key traits:**
- Kingdoms and empires
- Laws and governance
- Hierarchies

**Builders:** Cities, infrastructure, writing systems, administration

**Coordinators:** Kings, bureaucracies, military organization

**Skills needed:** Writing, reading, arithmetic, governance, law-making, military strategy, diplomacy, taxation and accounting

**Tools needed:** Writing systems (clay tablets, papyrus), administrative records, roads, fortifications, weapons and armor, measurement systems

**Obsoleted by next age:** Scribes (printing/machines replace manual copying); artisan craft at mass scale; manual calculation

**Outcome:** Large-scale societies possible → New constraint: labor productivity

---

## Age 4 — Industrial Age

**Primary scarcity:** Labor efficiency and production capacity

**Key traits:**
- Mass production
- Urbanization
- Wage labor

**Builders:** Machines, factories, steam power → electricity, assembly lines

**Coordinators:** Managers, corporations

**Skills needed:** Machine operation, mechanical engineering, standardization, bookkeeping, management, reading technical manuals, punctuality and shift discipline

**Tools needed:** Steam engines, factories, railways, assembly lines, electricity, precision measurement instruments

**Obsoleted by next age:** Steam-only engineers; telegraph operators; manual typesetting; hand-loom weaving at scale

**Outcome:** Massive productivity boost, goods become abundant → New constraint: energy + capital

---

## Age 5 — Energy / Infrastructure Age

**Primary scarcity:** Energy and systems to distribute it

**Key traits:**
- Global trade networks
- Transportation systems
- Electrification

**Builders:** Power grids, oil/electricity, logistics networks

**Coordinators:** Nation-states, global trade systems

**Skills needed:** Civil and electrical engineering, logistics planning, finance and investment, international negotiation, large-scale project management

**Tools needed:** Power grids, oil infrastructure, shipping vessels, railways, telegraphy and early telecommunications

**Obsoleted by next age:** Physical filing systems; memorization as primary knowledge store; card catalogs; fax machines

**Outcome:** Scale becomes global → New constraint: information and coordination

---

## Age 6 — Information Age

**Primary scarcity:** Information access and processing

**Key traits:**
- Digital economy
- Knowledge work
- Global connectivity

**Builders:** Computers, internet, software

**Coordinators:** Platforms (algorithms, networks)

**Skills needed:** Programming, data analysis, digital literacy, search and research, critical thinking, communication at scale, systems design

**Tools needed:** Computers, internet, software applications, databases, search engines, mobile devices

**Obsoleted by next age:** Rote coding; data entry; search-based research; memorizing syntax — AI absorbs execution-layer tasks

**Outcome:** Information becomes abundant → New constraint: attention → deeper: alignment

---

## Age 7 — Alignment Age (Current)

**Primary scarcity:** Coherent action

Not lack of knowledge — lack of aligned execution.

**Key traits:**
- Infinite content, limited focus
- Massive capability gap vs. execution gap
- Misaligned incentives everywhere

**Builders (today):** AI systems, automation tools, execution engines

**Coordinators (today):** Product leaders, incentive designers, algorithmic coordinators

**Skills needed:** Systems thinking, judgment and prioritization, AI prompting and orchestration, meaning-making, taste (knowing what to build), emotional intelligence, synthesis across domains

**Tools needed:** AI assistants, automation frameworks, productivity systems, decision frameworks, no-code/low-code platforms

**Obsoleted by next age (emerging):** Manual dashboards; standalone software silos; single-domain specialists without meta-skills

**Outcome (in progress):** Capability is massive — coherence is weak

→ Next constraint emerging: **Direction / Meaning**

---

## Key Insight

> Every age solves "can we do it?" — then faces "can we organize it?"
>
> We are now at: "We can do almost anything… but we don't know what to do—or can't align to do it."
