## People Movers Through the Ages

| Age | People Mover Form | What They Did |
|---|---|---|
| Survival | Tribal leaders | Social bonds, group coordination |
| Agriculture | Property systems, early rulers | Organized land ownership |
| Civilization | Kings, bureaucracies | Governed large populations |
| Industrial | Managers, corporations | Organized labor at scale |
| Energy | Nation-states, trade systems | Coordinated global flows |
| Information | Platforms and algorithms | Aggregated users, directed attention |
| Alignment | Human incentive designers, product leaders | Aligning human + AI action (AI facilitates, humans coordinate) |

---

## The Layer Changes — The Function Does Not

Each age scales what People Movers must handle. The role never disappears.

> People Movers of today = alignment architects, incentive designers, algorithmic coordinators

---

## Law Behind People Movers

**Law 2: Humans Don't Auto-Align**

Interests differ. Attention differs. Incentives differ. This always requires People Movers.

---

## Why People Movers Are Currently Weak

Today's imbalance:
- Builders (AI, tools) → extremely strong
- People Movers (alignment systems) → weak

This is why everything feels chaotic despite massive capability.

## AI as Facilitator, Not People Mover

AI is not a People Mover. It is a facilitation tool that helps humans coordinate more effectively.

- The human remains the top-level coordinator
- AI can surface information, reduce friction, and surface misalignment — but humans decide and direct
- Algorithms that direct attention (platforms) are tools built and operated by human People Movers, not People Movers themselves

> A navigation app does not move people. A human moves people using a navigation app.
