# Model — Internal vs External Constraints

## The Shift

Earlier ages solved external problems. Current and future ages solve internal/systemic ones.

---

## External Constraints (Pre-Information Age)

Problems were outside the human mind:

- No food → grow it
- No energy → generate it
- No labor → build machines

These are:
- Measurable
- Objective
- Universally agreed problems

Progress felt: **linear, visible, collective** ("everyone benefits")

---

## Internal Constraints (Now)

Today's bottlenecks are inside humans and systems:

- Attention (focus, distraction)
- Judgment (what matters?)
- Meaning (why do anything?)
- Intelligence alignment (what should AI do?)

These are:
- Subjective
- Hard to measure
- Different for every individual

Progress feels: **non-linear, personal, often invisible**

---

## Abundance Creates a New Kind of Scarcity

| Earlier | Now |
|---|---|
| Scarcity = "not enough" | Scarcity = "too much" |

Too much information. Too many choices. Too many opinions.

So instead of "we solved the problem" → it feels like "the problem exploded."

---

## The Feedback Loop Is Broken

Earlier systems had clear feedback:
- Farm → more crops → success
- Factory → more output → success

Now:
- Scroll 5 hours → feels productive but isn't
- Consume info → illusion of learning
- AI helps → but can reduce deep thinking

> Effort ≠ Progress anymore

That mismatch feels like stagnation.

---

## Progress Is No Longer Shared Equally

**Industrial Age:** If machines improve → everyone produces more

**Information/AI Age:** If intelligence tools improve → only people who use them well benefit

Result: Highly leveraged individuals → massive output. Others → overwhelmed, distracted.

---

## A Better Frame

Instead of: "We're not progressing like before"

Think: **"We've moved from solving physics → to solving psychology"**

---

## Key Insight

> Earlier ages improved what we can do.
> This age challenges how we think.
>
> Improving thinking is: slower, uneven, harder to see.

---

## Implication

The metric of progress is unclear:
- More information? → already too much
- More productivity? → at what cost
- More AI? → depends on usage

We lack a clear scoreboard for this age.
