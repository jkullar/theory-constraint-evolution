# Timeline — Ages of Human Progress

Each age is defined by one dominant scarcity. When that scarcity is solved, the next age begins.

> Old scarcities never disappear — they become cheaper, more reliable, less dominant.

---

## The Constraint Loop (Per Age)

Scarcity → Builders solve → Scale increases → Coordination breaks → People Movers organize → New scarcity emerges

---

## Age Summary Table

| Age | Primary Scarcity | Solved By | Next Scarcity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Survival | Food, water, shelter | Tools, fire | Land |
| Agriculture | Productive land | Farming, irrigation | Organization |
| Civilization | Control of people | Governance, writing | Labor efficiency |
| Industrial | Labor efficiency | Machines, factories | Energy + scale |
| Energy | Energy & distribution | Power grids, infrastructure | Information |

| Alignment | Coherent action | *(in progress)* | Direction / Meaning |

---

## Age 1 — Survival (Hunter-Gatherer Era)

**Primary scarcity:** Food, water, shelter

**Key traits:**
- Nomadic life
- Constant uncertainty
- No surplus

**Builders:** Fire, basic tools (stone, bone)

**People Movers:** Tribal leaders, social bonds

**Outcome:** Survival stabilized → New constraint: control over land

---

## Age 2 — Agricultural Age

**Primary scarcity:** Productive land

**Key traits:**
- Settlements and villages
- Food surplus begins
- Property and ownership emerge

**Builders:** Farming systems, irrigation, domestication of animals

**People Movers:** Property systems, early governance

**Outcome:** Food scarcity reduced, population increases → New constraint: organization at scale

---

## Age 3 — Civilization / Land-Control Age

**Primary scarcity:** Control of land and people

**Key traits:**
- Kingdoms and empires
- Laws and governance
- Hierarchies

**Builders:** Cities, infrastructure, writing systems, administration

**People Movers:** Kings, bureaucracies, military organization

**Outcome:** Large-scale societies possible → New constraint: labor productivity

---

## Age 4 — Industrial Age

**Primary scarcity:** Labor efficiency and production capacity

**Key traits:**
- Mass production
- Urbanization
- Wage labor

**Builders:** Machines, factories, steam power → electricity, assembly lines

**People Movers:** Managers, corporations

**Outcome:** Massive productivity boost, goods become abundant → New constraint: energy + capital

---

## Age 5 — Energy / Infrastructure Age

**Primary scarcity:** Energy and systems to distribute it

**Key traits:**
- Global trade networks
- Transportation systems
- Electrification

**Builders:** Power grids, oil/electricity, logistics networks

**People Movers:** Nation-states, global trade systems

**Outcome:** Scale becomes global → New constraint: information and coordination

---

## Age 6 — Information Age

**Primary scarcity:** Information access and processing

**Key traits:**
- Digital economy
- Knowledge work
- Global connectivity

**Builders:** Computers, internet, software

**People Movers:** Platforms (algorithms, networks)

**Outcome:** Information becomes abundant → New constraint: attention → deeper: alignment

---

## Age 7 — Alignment Age (Current)

**Primary scarcity:** Coherent action

Not lack of knowledge — lack of aligned execution.

**Key traits:**
- Infinite content, limited focus
- Massive capability gap vs. execution gap
- Misaligned incentives everywhere

**Builders (today):** AI systems, automation tools, execution engines

**People Movers (today):** Product leaders, incentive designers, algorithmic coordinators

**Outcome (in progress):** Capability is massive — coherence is weak

→ Next constraint emerging: **Direction / Meaning**

---

## Key Insight

> Every age solves "can we do it?" — then faces "can we organize it?"
>
> We are now at: "We can do almost anything… but we don't know what to do—or can't align to do it."
